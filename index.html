National Alliance on Mental Illness of Orange County (NAMI OC)

Contact person: Abby Nemeth
Phone: 714-544-8488
Website: www.namioc.org/
E-mail: info@namioc.org
Address: 1810 East 17th Street Santa Ana, CA. 92705
Hours: Office is open Monday - Friday, 9am - 5pm
Fees: no fees
Services: provides Zoom support groups, a peer connector program for mentorship, and OC Warmline (a 24/7 telephone service providing emotional support)
District partnership: Unknown

About

For students or parents who are in need of additional emotional support, contact Abby Nemeth at NAMI OC. NAMI OC provides residents of Orange County a variety of free and accessible emotional support options such as opportunities for Zoom support groups, a Peer Connector Program for mentorship, and a 24/7 free and confidential telephone service for emotional support (OC Warmline). The staff at NAMI OC rely on its event managers for planning, however much of the support comes from volunteers that help with providing emotional support through the OC Warmline. Services are provided in English, Arabic, Farsi, Korean, Spanish, Vietnamese, and Mandarin. There are no fees or costs associated with receiving assistance from NAMI OC. There are no referral services, however students and adults who are in need of accessible emotional support are encouraged to seek out NAMI OC's free resources. Overall, NAMI OC is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community. The Warmline may take a while to connect to someone at times, as staffing is limited. Having a backup support is highly recommended.



Western Youth Services

Contact person: Camille Kallas
Phone: 888-312-0406
Website: www.westernyouthservices.org/
E-mail: info@westernyouthservices.org
Address: vary locations
Hours: Monday-Friday 8am-5pm
Fees: depends on insurance
Services: outpatient mental health services for Medi-Cal eligible clients up to age twenty-one years old
District partnership: yes, AUHSD

About

Youth below the age of 21 looking for low-cost mental health services, contact Camille Kallas at Western Youth Services. Western Youth Services offers therapy for students from low-income families through their Medi-Cal outpatient clinics, along with providing crisis intervention trainings, and school-based services. Western Youth Services' staffing consists of Psychologists, Psychiatric/Mental health nurse practitioners, and Licensed Clinicians. A bachelors is required to apply, and proper licensing is necesasry depending on the employement role. Hours of service are Mondays to Fridays, 8am to 5pm. Western Youth Services accomodates for English, Spanish, Farsi, Korean, Vietnamese, Arabic, and Mandarin. Costs of services depend on if a youth has Medi-Cal, CalOptima, or any other form of insurance. To make a referral, either call 838-312-0406 or email gethelp@westernyouthservices.org. Western Youth Services is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.




Orange County Asian and Pacifica Islanders Community Alliance (OCAPICA)

Contact person: Rebecca Park
Phone: 714-636-6286
Website: www.ocapica.org/mentalhealth.html
E-mail: ocapica@ocapica.org
Address: 12912 Brookhurst St., Suite #480, Garden Grove, CA 92840
Hours: Office open Monday-Thursdays 8am-5:30pm
Fees: yes for therapy/counseling services, free workshops available
Services: provides mental health workshops, mental health services, and enrichment opportunities for Orange County's Asian and Pacific Islander community
District partnership: yes, AUHSD

About

For Orange County residents from the Asian and Pacific Islander community in need of mental health support, contact Rebecca Park at OCAPICA. OCAPICA's Well(ness)essity program offers short-term case management, short-term counseling, mental health screenings, educational workshops, and free consultations for emotional support and resource help. OCAPICA's staff consists of personal services coordinators, housing resource partners, case managers, and workforce support assistant. Additional support through volunteers and interns are also greatly appreciated. OCAPICA's offices are open from Monday to Fridays, 8am to 5:30pm. OCAPICA provides service in English, Korean, and Vietnamese. OCAPICA takes CalOptima and other forms of insurance for the paid, mental health services. Referrals can be made through this form (here). Because of the large and growing population of AAPI communities in Orange County, OCAPICA's is both a culturally appropriate and important resource.

OC Rescue Mission

Website: www.rescuemission.org
E-mail: admissions@rescuemission.org
Address: 1 Hope Dr, Tustin 92782
Hours: Monday - Friday, 8am - 4pm
Fees: no fees
Services: provides housing for homeless, and services such as meals, clothing, medical support, counseling, life skills, education, etc. for residents
District partnership: Unknown

About

For students who are homeless or are at risk of homelessness, contact Latisha Marshall at OC Rescue Mission. OC Rescue Mission provides meals, clothing, medical care, life skills, counseling, and more to its residents. The staff consist of a case manager, front desk workers, county worker, and a sizable population of volunteers who aid in the kitchen, warehouses, and in childcare. OC Rescue Mission is available Mondays to Fridays from 8am to 4pm. As of now, they only provide services in English. There are no fees or costs associated with receiving assistance from OC Rescue Mission. There are no referral services, however students and adults can walk in for their first intake pre-screening. Aside from the lack of language accomodations, OC Rescue Mission is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.

About

For teens in middle school to high school with a drive for academic success regardless of background, contact Briana Ramirez at BHGH SoCal. BHGH is a residential program that offers support, resources, and connections. BHGH aims to support those from troubled, low-income and guide them towards success into secondary education. Higher Ground OC

Phone: 714-833-5087
Website: www.highergroundoc.org/teenprogram
E-mail: info@highergroundoc.org
Address: 1535 E. Broadway, Anaheim, CA 92805
Hours: Wednesdays, 3:30pm-6pm
Fees: no fees
Services: the teen community is a free, weekly afterschool program with mentor support
District partnership: partnered with Lincoln Elementary

About

For those looking for a free afterschool program for their teen, contact Alexis Almaguer at Higher Ground. Higher Ground OC's Teen Program holds weekly afterschool sessions that offer activities and learning opportunities with a goal of developing the teens to have excellent social, cognitive, creative, vocational and citizenship comptence. The teen program also offers a mentorship program for teens to gain support from college students and Higher Ground staff. Higher Ground OC encourages staff that have experience with working with K-12 students. A GED or high school diploma is required to apply. Training in risk-assessment and handling teens is also provided for staff to grow familiar with the position. The teen program runs every Wednesdays from 3:30pm to 6:00pm. There are language accomodations for both English and Spanish. Higher Ground OC is also looking into integrating sign language support as well. There are no fees or costs associated with receiving assistance from Higher Ground OC's Teen Program. Schools may refer students who need extra service and care, however students and parents may also join through contacting the program. Higher Ground OC's Teen Program is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.

Hours: Mon-Thurs 7am-6pm, Fri 7am - 5pm, Sat-Sun 6:30am-4:30pm
Fees: slide-scale based
Services: provides affordable reproductive health care
District partnership: Unknown

About

For students or parents in need of either affordable reproductive health care or reproductive health education, contact Karen Alvarado at Planned Parenthood of Anaheim. Planned Parenthood provides services such as birth control, HIV services, contraceptiption, pregnancy testing, hormone therapy, and more for at an affordable price. Planned Parenthood is especially useful for those facing financial difficulties as they will work with both low-income or no-income. The staff at Planned Parenthood consists of medical assistants, reproductive health specialists, and clinicians. A degree and proper licensing is required to apply to be a mdical staff at Planned Parenthood. Planned Parenthood is available on Monday to Thursday from 7am to 6pm, Friday from 7am to 5pm, and Saturday to Sunday from 6:30am to 4:30pm. Planned Parenthood provides language assistance in English and Spanish. Costs are sliding scale-based. Those without insurance or Medicaid may still be eligible for state-funding or a lower fee sclae. Planned Parenthood does not have a referral system, however those in need of reproductive health services or education are encouraged to seek support. Planned Parenthood is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.
