OC Rescue Mission

Contact person: Latisha Marshall
Phone: 1-800-663-3074 or 714-247-4379
Website: www.rescuemission.org
E-mail: admissions@rescuemission.org
Address: 1 Hope Dr, Tustin 92782
Hours: Monday - Friday, 8am - 4pm
Fees: no fees
Services: provides housing for homeless, and services such as meals, clothing, medical support, counseling, life skills, education, etc. for residents
District partnership: none



About

For students who are homeless or are at risk of homelessness, contact Latisha Marshall at OC Rescue Mission. OC Rescue Mission provides meals, clothing, medical care, life skills, counseling, and more to its residents. The staff consist of a case manager, front desk workers, county worker, and a sizable population of volunteers who aid in the kitchen, warehouses, and in childcare. OC Rescue Mission is available Mondays to Fridays from 8am to 4pm. As of now, they only provide services in English. There are no fees or costs associated with receiving assistance from OC Rescue Mission. There are no referral services, however students and adults can walk in for their first intake pre-screening. Aside from the lack of language accomodations, OC Rescue Mission is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.

For families who are struggling or in need of food resources, contact Malia Cary at Second Harvest Food Bank. Second Harvest Food Bank hosts multiple food distributions a month for families in need of extra food resources. Staffing at Second Harvest Food Bank are mostly volunteers, accepting applicants from as young as 13 years old to aid in the farms or at the food distribution events. The distribution days and hours are dependent of each school site (check here). As of now, they only provide services in English, however volunteers may provide additional language support. There are no fees or costs associated with receiving assistance from Second Harvest Food Bank. There are no referral services, but parents and students are encouraged to check the distribution schedules if they are in need. Second Harvest Food Bank is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.

About

For teens in middle school to high school with a drive for academic success regardless of background, contact Briana Ramirez at BHGH SoCal. BHGH is a residential program that offers support, resources, and connections. BHGH aims to support those from troubled, low-income and guide them towards success into secondary education. BHGH staffing consists of Residential Counselors and Academic Counselors. A high school education or equivalent is required to apply, and experience working with youth is preferred. Volunteers for tutors and mentors are also greatly appreciated. As it is a residential program, scholars will be mostly staying in the provided housing and allowed home visits. BHGH accomodates for both English and Spanish. There are no fees or costs associated with receiving services from BHGH. Referrals can be made on the website (here). As it allows academic opportunities targeting those from low-income backgrounds, BHGH is both a culturally appropriate and important resource for the community.
